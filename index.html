Shell Method

V = ∫ 2π · x · f(x) dx
Wraps the solid in nested cylindrical shells around the z-axis. Each shell's circumference times its height times its thickness equals its volume — sum them and you have the integral.

Washer Method

V = π ∫ (f(x)² − g(x)²) dx
Slices the solid into thin disks (or rings) perpendicular to the x-axis. Each washer's outer radius is f(x), inner radius is g(x), and you stack them like coins to get the volume.
